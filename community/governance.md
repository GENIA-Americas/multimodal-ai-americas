# Governance

AI Corridor of the Americas
GENIA Americas Corporation / RaceFor.AI

---

## Overview

This document describes how the AI Corridor of the Americas is
governed: who makes decisions, how standards are set, how disputes
are resolved, and how the network evolves over time.

The governance model is federated. It is designed to preserve national
sovereignty while enabling regional coordination. Neither efficiency
nor legitimacy is sacrificed for the other — but where they conflict,
legitimacy takes precedence.

---

## Governing Bodies

### Network Council

The Network Council is the strategic governing body of the Corridor.

Composition:
- One representative per participating nation (designated by the
  national member organization)
- Three at-large seats elected by the working groups
- GENIA Americas Corporation holds a non-voting facilitation role

Responsibilities:
- Setting the Corridor's strategic direction
- Ratifying technical and ethical standards
- Approving new member organizations
- Resolving escalated disputes
- Overseeing the annual Race For AI Summit

Meeting cadence: quarterly, with the annual Summit serving as the
primary in-person convening.

Decision process: consensus is the default. Where consensus cannot
be reached, decisions require a two-thirds supermajority of
represented nations. Nations may abstain without blocking.

### Technical Working Groups

Working groups are the operational layer of the Corridor. They develop
standards, review research, and coordinate sector-specific programs.

Current working groups:

| Working Group | Scope | Chair |
|---------------|-------|-------|
| NLP and Language | Multilingual AI, language benchmarks | Open |
| Agricultural AI | Agri data standards, crop models | Open |
| AI Governance and Policy | Regulatory frameworks, compliance | Open |
| Platform and Infrastructure | Glapagos standards, API specs | GENIA Americas |
| Ethics and Fairness | Bias auditing, ethics review | Open |
| Financial AI | Fintech, financial inclusion | Open |
| Public Sector AI | Government systems, civic AI | Open |

Working groups are open to any researcher, engineer, or practitioner
with relevant expertise. Membership does not require organizational
affiliation with GENIA Americas.

To join a working group: open an issue with the Working Group Request
template or contact admin@genia.ai.

Working group chairs are elected by working group members annually.
GENIA Americas holds the Platform and Infrastructure chair as the
platform maintainer.

---

### Municipal Lab Network

The Municipal Lab Network is the implementation layer of the Corridor
at the city level. Labs are established through formal agreements
between GENIA Americas and participating municipal governments.

Labs operate under their own local governance structures. The Network
Council sets minimum standards for data governance and ethical review
that all labs must meet. Labs report outcomes to the Network Council
annually.

---

### GENIA Americas Corporation

GENIA Americas is the operating entity that maintains the shared
infrastructure: the Glapagos platform, this repository, the RaceFor.AI
network, and the Corridor Secretariat function.

GENIA Americas does not control the Network Council or working groups.
Its role is to execute decisions made through those bodies, maintain
continuity across leadership transitions, and represent the Corridor
in external policy and partnership contexts.

## Standards Process

Technical and ethical standards follow a structured process:

1. **Proposal** — any working group member may propose a standard
   by opening an issue with the Standards Proposal template
2. **Working group review** — the relevant working group reviews
   the proposal over a 30-day comment period
3. **Public comment** — proposals that pass working group review
   are opened for 30-day public comment in the repository Discussions
4. **Network Council ratification** — the Network Council votes to
   adopt, return for revision, or reject
5. **Publication** — adopted standards are published in the relevant
   directory with version number and effective date

Standards are versioned. Deprecated standards are retained in the
repository with their deprecation date and the standard that
superseded them.

## Membership

### Organizational Membership

Organizations join the Corridor through a formal agreement with
GENIA Americas. Membership requires:

- Acceptance of the Ethics Charter (policy/ethics-charter.md)
- Designation of a Network Council representative
- Annual reporting on AI deployments within the Corridor
- Contribution to at least one working group

Membership is open to government agencies, academic institutions,
private sector organizations, NGOs, and multilateral bodies.

### Individual Participation

Individual researchers and practitioners participate through:

- Contributing to this repository (see CONTRIBUTING.md)
- Working group membership
- Race For AI Summit participation
- Peer review of research submissions

Individual participation does not require organizational membership.

## Dispute Resolution

Disputes within the Corridor follow a graduated process:

1. **Direct resolution** — parties attempt to resolve directly,
   with working group chair facilitation if needed
2. **Working group mediation** — the full working group reviews
   and recommends resolution
3. **Network Council arbitration** — the Council reviews and issues
   a binding decision

Disputes involving GENIA Americas as a party are resolved by the
Network Council without GENIA Americas participation in the vote.

## Amendments

This governance document may be amended by:

1. Proposal submitted to the Network Council
2. 30-day comment period open to all participants
3. Two-thirds supermajority vote of the Network Council

Amendments are versioned and all prior versions retained.
