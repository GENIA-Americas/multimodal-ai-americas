# Literature Review: State of AI in the Americas

GENIA Americas Corporation / RaceFor.AI
Last updated: May 2026

Curated review of academic and applied literature relevant to AI
development in the Americas. Organized by topic, updated as significant
work is published. Open access preferred; paywalled work noted.

## AI Governance and Policy

OECD (2019). Recommendation of the Council on Artificial Intelligence.
Primary multilateral AI governance framework. Note regional limitations.
https://legalinstruments.oecd.org/en/instruments/OECD-LEGAL-0449

UNESCO (2021). Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence.
First global normative AI ethics instrument, 193 member states.
https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000381137

WHO (2021). Ethics and Governance of Artificial Intelligence for Health.
Six core principles: transparency, inclusion, accountability,
non-maleficence, privacy, equity.
https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240029200

Inter-American Development Bank (2020). The IDB and Artificial
Intelligence: A Framework for the Region.
https://publications.iadb.org/en/idb-and-artificial-intelligence

## NLP and Language

Conneau et al. (2020). Unsupervised Cross-lingual Representation
Learning at Scale. XLM-RoBERTa. Primary multilingual baseline.
https://arxiv.org/abs/1911.02116

Mager et al. (2018). Challenges of Language Technologies for the
Indigenous Languages of the Americas.
https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.04291

## Federated Learning

McMahan et al. (2017). Communication-Efficient Learning of Deep Networks
from Decentralized Data. FedAvg. Foundation of federated learning.
https://arxiv.org/abs/1602.05629

Kairouz et al. (2021). Advances and Open Problems in Federated Learning.
Comprehensive survey, 58 authors. Essential reference.
https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.04977

## AI Fairness and Bias

Barocas, Hardt, Narayanan (2023). Fairness and Machine Learning.
Standard textbook reference for algorithmic fairness.
https://fairmlbook.org

Eubanks (2018). Automating Inequality. Critical analysis of AI in
public-sector contexts serving low-income populations. Essential for
public sector AI work in the Corridor.
