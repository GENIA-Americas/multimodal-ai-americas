# Regional AI Regulatory Frameworks

GENIA Americas Corporation / RaceFor.AI
Policy and Governance Division

Last reviewed: May 2026

---

## Purpose

This document maps the AI-relevant regulatory landscape across the
Americas. It is a living reference — updated as legislation evolves —
for researchers, engineers, and policymakers working within the
AI Corridor.

All entries include a last-reviewed date. Policy documents go stale.
Do not rely on this document for legal compliance without verifying
current status with qualified counsel in the relevant jurisdiction.

---

## Regulatory Categories

This document tracks four categories of regulation relevant to AI
deployment:

1. Data protection and privacy law
2. Sector-specific AI regulation (health, finance, public sector)
3. National AI strategy documents
4. Cross-border data flow agreements

---

## North America

### United States

**Data protection**
The U.S. has no comprehensive federal data protection law as of mid-2026.
Regulation is sectoral: HIPAA for health data, GLBA for financial data,
FERPA for education records, COPPA for children's data. Several states
have enacted comprehensive privacy laws, with California's CCPA/CPRA
the most significant.

**AI-specific regulation**
Executive Order 14110 (October 2023) established federal AI governance
requirements for agencies and set voluntary commitments for industry.
NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0, January 2023) provides
the de facto technical standard for U.S.-based AI risk management.
The EU AI Act does not apply in the U.S. but affects U.S. organizations
operating in or serving the EU market.

**Congressional engagement**
H.Res.649 (118th Congress) proposed a hemispheric AI strategy. See
policy/hres649.md for full analysis. Not enacted.

**AI strategy**
The National AI Initiative Act (2020) established the National AI
Initiative. The Biden administration's AI Bill of Rights (2022)
established non-binding principles for AI in consumer contexts.

**Implications for Corridor deployment**
Organizations deploying through the Corridor into U.S. markets must
navigate state-level patchwork privacy law. Glapagos compliance
modules address CCPA/CPRA and major state frameworks.

### Mexico

**Data protection**
Ley Federal de Proteccion de Datos Personales en Posesion de los
Particulares (LFPDPPP, 2010) governs private-sector data. Ley General
de Proteccion de Datos Personales en Posesion de Sujetos Obligados
(2017) covers public sector. Enforcement by the INAI.

**AI strategy**
Mexico has not enacted a comprehensive national AI strategy as of
mid-2026. Sector-level AI adoption is advancing in fintech (CNBV
oversight) and health (COFEPRIS jurisdiction).

**Implications for Corridor deployment**
Mexico is a priority market for the Corridor given its industrial base
in manufacturing, logistics, and fintech. LFPDPPP compliance is
required. No AI-specific licensing requirements currently in force.

---

### Canada

**Data protection**
The Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act
(PIPEDA) is the federal framework. Bill C-27, which would enact the
Consumer Privacy Protection Act (CPPA) and the Artificial Intelligence
and Data Act (AIDA), has been under consideration. Verify current
legislative status.

**AI strategy**
Canada's Pan-Canadian Artificial Intelligence Strategy (2017, renewed
2021) established the national AI research and commercialization
framework. The Directive on Automated Decision-Making applies to
federal government AI systems.

## Central America and the Caribbean

### Panama

Relevant as a logistics and financial hub for the Corridor. Data
protection under Law 81 of 2019. No AI-specific regulation in force.
AI applications in logistics and supply chain management are a
priority deployment area.

### Costa Rica

Strong institutional framework and digital government agenda. Ley de
Proteccion de la Persona frente al Tratamiento de sus Datos Personales
(Law 8968). AI in public-sector modernization is an active area.

### Regional note

Most Central American and Caribbean nations are in early stages of
data protection legislation. Several are developing national AI
strategies. Corridor engagement in this sub-region focuses on
municipal AI labs and public-sector modernization as entry points.

## South America

### Brazil

**Data protection**
Lei Geral de Protecao de Dados (LGPD, Law 13.709/2018) is the
comprehensive data protection framework, modeled on but distinct
from the EU's GDPR. Enforcement by the Autoridade Nacional de
Protecao de Dados (ANPD). Brazil is the most developed data
protection regime in the region.

**AI regulation**
Brazil's AI Bill (PL 2338/2023) proposes a risk-based AI regulation
framework. As of mid-2026, verify current legislative status. The
Senate AI Commission report (2023) provides the policy foundation.

**AI strategy**
Brazil's National AI Strategy (EBIA, 2021) identifies priority sectors
including agriculture, health, and public security.

**Implications for Corridor deployment**
Brazil is the largest market in the hemisphere and the most
sophisticated regulatory environment in Latin America. LGPD compliance
is non-negotiable. The Glapagos platform includes dedicated LGPD
compliance modules. Brazil is a priority for the agribusiness,
fintech, and supply chain verticals.

---

### Argentina

**Data protection**
Law 25.326 (Personal Data Protection Act, 2000) is the current
framework, widely considered outdated relative to modern AI deployment
requirements. Reform discussions ongoing.

**AI strategy**
Argentina's National AI Plan was published in 2019. The country has
significant AI research capacity concentrated in Buenos Aires.

**Implications for Corridor deployment**
Strong technical talent base. Regulatory environment in transition.
Active Corridor engagement in agribusiness and fintech sectors.

---

### Colombia

**Data protection**
Law 1581 of 2012 and Decree 1377 of 2013 govern data protection.
Superintendencia de Industria y Comercio (SIC) is the enforcement body.

**AI strategy**
Colombia published a national AI policy document (CONPES 3975, 2019)
establishing a framework for AI adoption across government and industry.

**Implications for Corridor deployment**
Colombia is a priority for the energy and fintech verticals. Medellin
and Bogota are active tech hubs with established AI communities.

---

### Chile

**Data protection**
Law 19.628 (1999) is the current framework. A comprehensive data
protection reform has been under development. Verify current status.

**AI strategy**
Chile's National AI Policy (2021) establishes principles for responsible
AI development. Strong institutional capacity in the mining and energy
sectors creates direct AI application opportunities.

**Implications for Corridor deployment**
Priority for mining AI and energy optimization applications. Strong
technical institutions including Universidad de Chile and PUC.

---

### Peru and Bolivia

Emerging regulatory environments. Data protection frameworks in
development. Significant AI opportunity in mining, agriculture, and
biodiversity modeling. Active Corridor engagement at the working group
level.

## Cross-Border Frameworks

### Ibero-American Data Protection Network

The Red Iberoamericana de Proteccion de Datos (RIPD) coordinates
data protection standards across Spanish and Portuguese-speaking
nations. Membership includes most South American nations and Spain.
Relevant for cross-border data flows within the region.

### OAS AI Working Group

The Organization of American States has active working groups on
cybersecurity and digital governance with AI dimensions. Corridor
engagement ongoing.

### Trade agreements with digital chapters

USMCA (2020) includes digital trade provisions relevant to AI-related
data flows between the U.S., Mexico, and Canada. CPTPP digital trade
provisions apply to Chile, Mexico, Peru, and Canada among Americas
signatories.
